Best places to eat in accra

Find out where the best restaurants are in Accra for authentic Ghanaian food...

Accra's Top Restaurants!

Aunti Manni

Aunti Manni is a weekend food stall which is renowned for its waakye. The food stall attracts former presidents, football stars and countless other Ghanaians. Open on weekends and public holidays, patrons can be found forming a queue at Auntie Manni’s as early as 7am. Auntie Manni’s business has skyrocketed from a small venture to a distinguished food service.

Labone District, Accra

Frankie's Foods and Rooms

Frankie's is located on Oxford Street in the neighbourhood of Osu. It is one of the oldest fast-food restaurants in Accra. This fantastic restaurant offers an array of dishes from continential to Lebanese food. However, Frankie's is very well known for its jollof rice which is a must try. Frankie's is open till midnight making a great place for evening meals.

Oxford Street, Osu

Chez Clarisse

Chez Clarisse is a popular Ivorian restaurant in the Osu neighbourhood of Accra. This small restaurant doesn't make it any less popular. One of the best grilled tilapia and banku dishes is served at the Ivorian restaurant alongside grilled chicken. This real African food experience includes plastic chairs, eating with your hands and sauces that keep fans coming back.

8th Ln, Osu

Buka

Situated in the centre of Osu, Buka is a popular hot spot in Accra. Set on the first floor, it gives a sense of escape from the throng, with lively music and wraparound wooden trellises adding to the ambience. The food itself hinges largely on Ghanaian and Nigerian dishes. Popular dishes in this lively restaurant include jollof rice, tilapia and groundnut soup.

10th Ln, Osu

Republic Bar & Grill

The Republic is a must visit when visiting Accra. It is one of the most popular bars in Accra thanks to its relaxed atmosphere, great food and amazing cocktail drinks. You can sample some indigenous Ghanaian dishes and listen to traditional music. These authentic dishes that the Republic serve are not only delicious but also very affordable.

3rd Lane, Oxford Street

Bistro 22

Bistro 22 is a perfect restaurant for lunch, dinner, coffee or even drinks. It pays special attention to the quality of its beef which is why their gourmet burger are a popular choice. In addition to their special beef cuts, their popular dishes include milanesa, rosemary chicken and chicken satay. A new leafy terrace out the back is the perfect spot for an evening dinner.

22 Josiah Tongogari, Labone

Urban Grill

Urban Grill is an upscale restaurant that only serves food at the highest quality. Not only does it serve food at the highest standard, but the service is also top notch. he meat is corn-fed and imported directly from Nebraska, then cooked over a charcoal grill to perfection. Many reviewers have rated Urban Grill as the restaurant to go for the best Steak in all of Accra.

Icon House, North Liberation Link

Firefly Lounge Bar

Firefly is a lounge, bar and restaurant situated in Accra’s lively entertainment district, Osu. The menu is a fusion of African and Mediterranean cuisine including a variety of tapas and pasta dishes, salads, and heartier mains such as stuffed chicken breast, steak medallions and calamari. In addition to the fantastic food, the drink menu is also extensive and top quality.

11th Lane, Embassy Road

Tea Baa

This lively little joint specialises in iced tea (both alcoholic and non-alcoholic) served in hipster-friendly glass jars. It is Ghana's first tea bar and is also one of the only tea bars in Accra. The food is some of the best in Accra – there are three or so options per day, cooked in front of you on the open-air grill. The ambiance is incredible with both staff and customers happy to chat.

Klannaa Street, Accra
