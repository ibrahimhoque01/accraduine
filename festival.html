Accra's Top Festivals

Homowo Festival - May

Homowo festival is arguably one of the biggest festivals in all of Ghana. It happens every May and is celebrated by the Ga people from the Greater Accra Region of Ghana. Homowo is a harvest festival of plant food and fish as such homowo referring to the act of ‘hooting at hunger’ in remembrance of hunger famine that once happened in their history. During the festival a ban is placed on all music and noise by the Ga Traditional Council (GTC).

Chale Wote Festival - August

The Chale Wote festival takes place every August and is one of the biggest festivals in Ghana. It attracts thousands of people for a week of festivites. These festivities include film premieres, gallery shows, music, food and dance. The festival takes place in the historic neighbourhood of Jamestown which was used as a port for movement during days of exploitation and systematization of colonialism. This festival is very capitivating, bright and full of life.

PANAFEST - July/August

Also known as the Pan-African Historical Theatre festival, is an event dedicated to African dance, music and other performing arts. This festival showcases aspects historical and cultural legacy and endowment. It was started in the mid 1980s as a project to influence homecoming for Africans all around the world. Since then, it takes place every two years to celebrate the people of African descent as well as all persons committed to the well being of Africans on continent and in the global arena.

Accra Food Festival - September

Food lovers from Accra and worldwide meet to check out the mouth-watering plates in variegated tasting tents for affordable prices. Traditional and non-traditional cuisines serve the best of food and drinks from Ghana and other parts of the world in a pleasant ambiance. With its great atmosphere and live entertainment, the Accra Food Festival is perfect for all ages.

Afrochella - December/January

Thousands of people, not just from Africa but from around the world, gather at the Afrochella festival in Accra. This festival is not different to Coachella in California USA. However, in Afrochella, African culture and diversity is celebrated through the music, food and live performances this festival has to offer. Afrochella is a great way to explore Ghana and is a definite must visit if one is visiting Accra when the festival is taking place.
